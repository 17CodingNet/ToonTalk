4. Can you describe sequences that can't be programmed?

 

We cannot describe any but we are pretty sure that they do exist and may have something to do with irrational numbers.

 

S (13-year old girl), T (13-year old boy), and A (13-year old boy) discussed this before writing their answer. They took the “quizmanship” position that the question wouldn’t be asked if there were no such sequences. S after a while suggested that maybe there are such sequences but they can’t be described. T suggested sequences like “one plus the number in my head” but the others rejected that and he agreed. A suggested sequences like musical symphonies and the others said it had to be a number sequence. A then wondered about the digits of pi and whether that was a sequence and whether it can be programmed. T then searched the web for formulas for computing pi. Someone suggested a sequence using pi (I think pi, 2pi, 3pi, …) and they wondered if computers can deal with irrational numbers. But they thought probably not.
