Activity 2: Combining infinite sequences – part 1,  in order to answer the question: 

Are the integers more than the naturals?

Children train these robots:

  1. Negator that multiplies its input by -1
  2. Merge that shuffles two streams into one

In Sofia:

Ivan:...Then I constructed a robot which merged the negative and the positive numbers. But 0 was missing in its output. ... I suggested to start ADD1 with –1 as a first member, and Yana decided to put 0 in the output nest of MERGE first. Finally I constructed MERGE1 which combined the natural numbers with all the integers alternatively. In my first version of MERGE1 the birds carrying the natural numbers and the integers were not synchronised so I decided to retrain it. In the last version MERGE1 was connecting each dancing pair of integers  and natural numbers in a 2-hole box and giving this pair to the output bird.... So now it is clear that the integers are as many as the natural numbers!!!


In Plovdiv (with the group of 3d  graders):

Teacher:  Let’s check what would Negator do if you give him the number –10 for example. Do you expect him returning a number or getting angry?

Victor: He should get angry, he doesn’t know how to deal with numbers having an opposite sign.

Theodor: No, I think he will return 10...

Teacher: Why? We are giving him –10 and we have trained him to put a minus sign in front…

Theodor: Because he is clever, that’s why! I am not sure why but I still think that he could return 10. When we give him 5 it returns –5, puts the minus sign in front. Why shouldn’t he return to –10 its opposite, 10?

Comments by Weblabs Partners: 

 Sofia: ... Now that you have checked how your robot reacts to negative inputs can you say that

 -(-5) = +5 = 5

and more general that

  -(-number) = +number = number

 no matter which number you take?

 

Ken: Victor noticed you can't erase a number, type - and then type 5. ToonTalk was improved about 6 months ago to fix this problem. The reason it used to have problems with this is that ToonTalk wanted a number is always be a good number. After you type - but before you type 5 then you have a very strange number: just a minus sign! But the latest version of ToonTalk fixes this by allowing typing to make a number into nonsense and then fixing things if needed when it is dropped.

Do you know what it means to multiply a number by -1?

 

Note1: The Sofia students figured out  that the Negator could produce positive numbers provided that the input was negative. For tasks that seem time-consuming (e.g. emptying a nest with "too many numbers" in it) Ivan trained a robot to do it. The need for refinement  and enrichment of their robots is very rewarding thing to observe... Note also their sense of humor when naming the cells - the nest is called NESTLE :) Note also that this time Ivan sounds more convincing in answering the initial challenge now that he has a constructive proof that the integers are as many as the naturals!

Note 2: Even though the mathematical questions we address are very challenging we never omit the chance also to explore more modest mathematical topics, like manipulating with negative numbers. The 3d graders were lacking  preliminary experience with negative numbers but after exploration with negative inputs of their robot they realized that it does return the opposite number of the input number. The comments by the Weblabs researchers  helped them overcome their initial irritation that the "minus" should be entered after the number, and more important - to generalise their founding that -(-10) is 10. In addition they realised the double role of the "minus sign" - as a unary and binary operation.
