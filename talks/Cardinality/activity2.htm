Combining infinite sequences – part 1

Children train these robots:

  1. Negator that multiplies its input by -1
  2. Merge that shuffles two streams into one

S said that if the natural numbers paired up with the positive integers then all the negative integers would be left over. I suggested there were other pairings where they match. I suggested she write down the current sequence

1, -1, 2, -2, 3, -3 and under it the natural numbers

1,  2, 3,  4, 5,  6

She pointed out that at any point only half as many positive (or negative) integers have been done as the natural numbers. I suggested she think about it and write down her thoughts. She wrote in response to the question of whether she could see a way to get all the natural numbers to dance with all the integers:

The numbers go on forever but for every natural number (1, 2, 3,) there are two integers (1, -1, -2, 2) so although you can write them out and they all pair up, every natural number is also one of the integers but there are twice as many integers.

Later she told me “It doesn’t make sense but it must be right” since running forever exhausts both lists.
