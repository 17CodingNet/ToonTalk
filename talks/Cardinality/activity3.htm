Computing a variety of infinite sequences

Children train these robots:

  1. Three infinite sequences of their choosing
  2. Match Maker that produces boxes describing who is "dancing" with whom

In response to

Will all the natural numbers have a different dancing partner [in the numbers in your sequence]?

S wrote, "Yes. But only if the original sequence has no repeated numbers, then some partners would be with 2 natural numbers."

S earlier observed that there are no duplicates when starting with different numbers since keep adding 1 to it and then do things to it, you’ll always get different numbers. She wrote that her sequence that squared and subtracted 20 had no duplicates

“because every time we start with a different number (1, 2, 3) the square -20, you still have different numbers”. [Impressive reasoning but not true if you square both negative numbers and positive ones.]

On the next session S came up with yet another argument why her sequences don’t have duplicates:

“it keeps going up forever”.
